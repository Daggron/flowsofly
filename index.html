I wonder where broken hearts go. Maybe they hide behind warm smiles and chirpy how are you’s or maybe they sulk in corners and or lies in the voice of your friend snapping because she is annoyed by the work of emotions. Maybe they don’t hide at all, maybe they stand right in front of you, ten feet tall and hover over you. They make it apparent so you’d sympathise and mitigate their aching with compassion etched in your supple palms. I wonder if a shattered heart holds the capacity to slice through skin because I’ve seen arms engraved with the initials of people’s lovers or if it’s competent to spread poison through someone’s body because I’ve seen hospital beds occupied by angst lovers who tried to poison themselves. I wonder where broken hearts hide. Maybe they hide in the tears that are persistent in the corner of the eyes or sprawled in beds with all the lights turned off. Maybe they lurk in the over-enthusiastic girl in your class who asks too many questions and focuses too much on her assignments to distract herself from her afflictions. I wonder if the lady standing next to me on the stop is broken hearted, she keeps sniffing but she says she’s just cold. I look at people and wonder how many times they fought and lost; how many times they fell and splintered & how many times they got up; how many times they were galant; how many times they were kind. If you’re reading this: I know you’ve been hurt & tormented but you’re here. You made it through with great fortitude & I want you to know I’m proud of you, I idolise you & I love you.
